General Body

Meetings and powers

  • The Annual General Meeting shall be held once every year.
  • In general years, the Annual General Meeting may be conducted in blended mode.
  • In election years, the Annual General Meeting shall be conducted offline.
  • Special General Meetings may be convened by the Executive Committee.
  • The General Body elects the Executive Committee.
  • The General Body approves policies, plans, and budgets.
  • The General Body receives and adopts the annual report and audited statement of accounts.
Executive Committee

Meetings and functions

  • The Executive Committee shall meet at least four times a year.
  • The President presides over meetings of the Society and represents the Society officially.
  • The Vice President assists the President and presides in the absence of the President.
  • The Secretary manages the day-to-day affairs of the Society and maintains records.
  • The Assistant Secretary assists the Secretary and acts in the absence of the Secretary.
  • The Treasurer maintains accounts and manages financial records of the Society.
  • Executive Members assist in organizing programmes and activities of the Society.
Election cycle

Tenure of office bearers

  • Office bearers are elected once every three years.
  • Elections are conducted during the Annual General Meeting.
  • A quorum of 25% of the General Body is required for valid elections.
  • No quorum is required for an adjourned meeting.
Eligibility

Requirements for office bearers

  • President: Life member with 15 years of experience.
  • Vice President: Life member with 10 years of experience.
  • Secretary / General Secretary: Life member with 10 years of experience.
  • Assistant Secretary: Life member with 10 years of experience.
  • Treasurer: Life member with 10 years of experience.
  • Executive Members: Annual members with continuous annual membership for three years.
Financial provisions

Funds and accounts

  • Funds of the Society may be raised through subscriptions, donations, gifts, or grants.
  • The Society shall maintain a bank account.
  • The bank account shall be jointly operated by the Secretary and Treasurer.
  • A qualified life member shall be appointed as Auditor at each Annual General Meeting.
Legal provisions

Amendment and dissolution

  • The Constitution or Memorandum may be amended by a three-fourths majority of members present at an Annual General Meeting.
  • A notice period of 21 days is required for amendment.
  • Proposed amendments shall be circulated to Executive Committee members prior to the meeting.
  • The Society may be dissolved only by a three-fourths majority of members present at a Special General Meeting convened for this purpose.
  • Upon dissolution, the assets of the Society shall be transferred to an organization with similar objectives.
  • Jurisdiction shall be confined to Meghalaya.
Membership discipline

Removal of membership

  • Any member may be expelled by the Executive Committee if found acting against the objectives of the Society.
  • The member shall have the right to appeal before the General Body.
