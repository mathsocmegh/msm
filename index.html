Mathematical Society of Meghalaya

Nurturing Mathematical Thinking Across Meghalaya

We are a community who believe Mathematics can thrive in Meghalaya not just in classrooms, but in everyday curiosity. Founded in September 2024, the Society is still young, but the ideas behind it have been a long time coming.

About the Society

Why we exist ?

Meghalaya has a vibrant community of students and teachers interested in Mathematics, but opportunities to come together and exchange ideas have been limited. The Society seeks to create a space where collaboration and academic dialogue can develop and strengthens the visibility of Mathematics in the region.

What we do ?

We bring people together through lectures, workshops, seminars, and annual gatherings where ideas can be shared and discussed openly. The aim is to make Mathematics feel less isolated and more like a living, ongoing conversation across the state.

Who are we ?

We are college teachers, university researchers, school students, and anyone with a genuine interest in Mathematics. The Society is open to all, whether you solve equations for a living or simply enjoy a good puzzle. What holds us together is a shared belief that Mathematics in Meghalaya deserves more attention and more voices.

Aims of the Society
  • To advance and promote the study and research of Mathematics in Meghalaya.
  • To popularize Mathematics through workshops, seminars, symposia, competitions, and related academic activities.
  • To publish journals, bulletins, and other periodicals for the dissemination of mathematical knowledge.
  • To secure and manage funds for the promotion of mathematical education and research.
Objectives of the Society
  • To organize an annual congress or conference of members of the Society.
  • To identify and encourage student talent in Mathematics.
  • To support and encourage teachers in the effective teaching of Mathematics.
  • To help reduce Mathematics anxiety and cultivate wider interest in the subject.
  • To function as a non-political academic body devoted to the advancement of Mathematics.
